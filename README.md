## The problem

Before the Brand Hub, Ambientha's team faced three recurring pain points:

- **Scattered logos** across folders, emails and different drives, every new piece started with a search
- **Color and tone inconsistencies** in published content, especially between stories, posts and printed materials
- **Slow onboarding** anyone new took weeks to understand the brand guidelines

The solution was to centralize everything in a single, accessible tool that takes care of the internal user experience as much as the end customer's.

---

## The project

As the person responsible for Ambientha's communication and as a frontend developer, I led this project end-to-end:

- **Brand strategy** — defining the tone of voice pillars and curating visual guidelines
- **Content** — full writing of the communication guide, response templates and usage rules
- **Design** — visual reference based on Ambientha's official website, keeping brand coherence
- **Development** — implementation in Next.js with focus on responsiveness and usability

---

## Features

- **Style Guide** — primary and complementary color palettes with one-click hex copy (no need to open Figma)
- **Brand Manual** — logo download with filters for color (black/white) and format (SVG/PNG), in four variations (horizontal, vertical, retraction and stamp)
- **Tone of Voice** — communication pillars, words to use and avoid, response templates for common situations and a quick replies table
- **Mobile-first** — fully responsive, designed for quick consultations on mobile during customer service

---

## Decisions and learnings

- **Next.js 16 with App Router** — scalable structure in case the team requests new sections in the future (product catalog, image bank, etc.)
- **Tailwind v4 + Geist Font** — neutral typography that doesn't compete with the brand's serif font, keeping focus on the content
- **Figma-integrated design** — used Figma to extract context directly from the design file and ensure visual fidelity
- **Filters + single click** — tested multiple selectors and bulk download buttons; the final version (global filter + click-to-download) proved more natural in actual use
